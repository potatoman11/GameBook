Game Reviews

1. Fortnite / Ratings:3.7/5

Fortnite is a cartoony third person shooter game which is especially good for people who do not like blood. Fortnite is on of the most streamed games ever and quickly rose to the top 10s. Fortnite is a game defending against zombie like figures and also their very popular mode, Battle Royale. Popular players include Twitch.BlooTea, Ninja and Sevennoss.Fortnite is run by epic games. You can play Fortnite on a Mac, PC, Nintendo, PS4 and Xbox.

2. The Witcher 3:Wild Hunt / Ratings:4.8/5

Witcher 3 is an online action Role Playing video game.Developed and published by CD Projekt, The Witcher 3 was based off of a series of novels by Polish author Andrzej Sapkowski. The Witcher 3 is played on a PS4 and is a sequel to The Witcher 2:Assasins of Kings. The Witcher can also be played on a Xbox and Microsoft Windows. In Third Person Perspective, players control Geralt of Rivia, a monster hunter known as a Witcher.

3. Minecraft / Ratings:4/5

Minecraft is an old game originally developed by Notch (aka Markus Persson) and later developed by Mojang. It is a sandbox game and began as a hobby. It allows players to build with an assortment of blocks. Other topics in the game include exploration, crafting and combat. By 2018, 144 million copies have been sold. Minecon conventions also add to the game's popularity. Minecraft can be played on Xbox, tablet, ipad, phone and a Mac.

4. Player Unknown's Battlegrounds (Pub G) / Ratings:4.3/5

Pub G is a Battle Royale game developed by PUB G Corporations. It is a third person shooter game and is similiar to Fortnite Battle Royale, except more realistic and not that cartoony. Pub G is based off of a last man standing concept, with 100 players going into a battle and one player winning. Pub G was released in 2017 for Steam on Windows, and later released for Xbox/ other devices. Popular players are Anthony_KongPhan, JackFrags and Lirik.

5. No Man's Sky / Ratings: 4/5

No Man's Sky is and action adventure survival game developed by the indie studio Hello Games. It was released worldwide on August 2016 for PlayStation 4 and Microsoft Windows. In No Man's Sky, players can perform in free space with over 18 quintillion planets waitinh to be explored. Planes even have their own ecosytems with their own unique forms of fauna and flora.
